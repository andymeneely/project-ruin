Cooperative with Micro-turns

Let’s go a little different. Make the game state a little harder to parse for others, and quick turns reduce analysis paralysis and therefore taking over.

Micro-turns means lot of turns with really short actions. If we assume a 4-person game in 90 minutes, then

Simultaneous? So we can have a more complex accounting phase where people can help each other out. But micro turns means things are quick. Yeah micro turns.

Cool downs on turns. Or some sort of energy system that lets you build up anticipation on making an epic move.

Engine building of some sort with lots of resources. Akin to Terraforming mars where you have cubes that represent "1 of" whatever they are one.

Opportunity Card

  • Resource cost on every card (e.g energy). Consumed by an action. Along side with big arrow to action text.
  • Required resources that are not consumed. Along bottom.
  • Title
  • Tags
  • Actions, e.g. Resource produced

A card can have multiple flows. Maybe 1 energy gets 3 and 2 energy gets 8. Or, the flow goes linearly where one action goes to another. For example, a "satellite" first is deployed, then each turn you can use it to uncover objectives with more energy.

Card can have a max storage if specified. Eg Max 5 ships.

First need to "charge" the ability by putting cubes on the energy spot. As soon as you satisfy the requirements, you may execute the action on the card.

Player-colored cubes? Allows us to track when things come from another player. Eh. Confusing.

Card actions trigger when the conversion is satisfied - even if it’s not your turn!!! This leads to some satisfying pinball moves down the line.

Faction ideas

  • American govt: good at military, bad at sharing cards, good at humans. Funding model is selling weapons.
  • Chinese govt: good at manufacturing, producing humans, bad at sharing cards, funding model based on manufacturing
  • Russian govt: also good at military, bad at sharing cards, funding model is based on hacking
  • Mega Corporation: potentially lots of money if you keep your profits up (think Amazon), buffs others. Has the ability to sell things but the market gets worse as secrecy lowers
  • Crowd of conspiracy theorists: cannot deploy soldiers, but great at research and producing human specialists, great at sharing (you can skip the funding step and instead give an opportunity to another player’s discard). Spreading energy out to one at each different card makes them better at more, cheaper cards (Crowdsource).
  • Private space transport: good at moving things around, (SpaceX), can reveal a new opportunity each turn or when another player takes an opportunity, good at recon
  • Space pirates: very little research, few humans, lots of good recon and high damage military
  • Religious society. Good at theologians and collaborating. Able to mitigate secrecy.

Funding model power: add a manufacturing good as part of your funding phase.

Buffs & Debuffs

In addition to cubes, you can also get "buffs". As a tile on a card or another card. Like "x2" where you can double the resources. Buffs stay until dispelled.

Buff ideas

  • Double resources produced
  • Add an energy back to the action each time
  • Give an energy cube to any player
  • Reduce energy cost on particular tags
  • When you do this action, you may discard an opportunity
  • Increase max storage
  • Transport buff: move as soon as you produce. Saves you an action of * transporting. "On-demand Logistics"
  • On funding, shuffle your deck. Brings advanced cards up.
  • Or, bring an advanced card up X cards from the bottom.

Debuffs

  • Cannot gain buffs. Dispel existing buffs on this
  • Higher energy requirement
  • Pass this debuff to someone else
  • Disable action
  • Fear: humans on a card are disabled
  • Breakdown: equipment fails to work until repaired
  • Set max storage on a card
  • Max buff stack on any card

Buff cards (tiles?) have a card-shaped requirement along bottom. Similar to existing resource. So you put the buff card behind the actual card.

  • So you start with a pattern of cards on the table

Alien quest deck

  • Gun batteries: needs 5 ships and a total of 20 damage. Mix of leaders and manufacturing and better tech.
  • Bombardment weapons. Need either defense or ships to overcome.
  • Pathogen labs: needs vaccine and treatment discovered. Mix of manufacturing and science.
  • Assault army: need home defenses or assault teams
  • Religious center: needs language, behavioral experts, and theologians to communicate. Maybe starts an event chain where you must get rid of some resources?
  • Spy center: discover how they learned about us. Secrecy decreases on discovery
  • Manufacturing plant: make goods for them, need a certain manufacturing capacity each turn. "Produce X goods each round for 3 rounds".
  • Hungry: need food for them, not really anything else but they need a lot so it’s about productivity.
  • Attempting to warn us about dangers. Need lots of translation.
  • Resource stockpile: give them resources they need, a couple of different types
  • Some quests can have a "when discovered" event, like "kill the recon squad" or "shoot down any satellites" or "assassinate all behavioralists" or "add energy to anyone with spaceships" or "remove any stored energy"
  • Back of the quest deck can have cubes for exploration difficulty you must remove first. This is randomized at the beginning of the game to add variety.
  • Not every card needs to be a quest. Some of them just have aliens in them as obstacles. Hostiles to go around or fight.

Project Secrecy

The project starts as SECRET, but over time the public becomes more aware of a problem. Decrease secrecy on certain card actions, and each round. When you run out, flip the card over and the endgame gets expedited.

Perhaps we have a Panic resource which is the Secrecy decrease per turn. And then card actions adjust secrecy up or down. We never add to secrecy, only adjust panic levels. Or prevent one round of secrecy decrease. Panic maps to secrecy decrease as a step function to make decisions more interesting.

  • Conspiracy theorists have moves that decrease secrecy a lot
  • Some govts are better at secrets. Eg China. US is not.
  • Private corporation "divert profits" to get more energy in a turn at the cost of some secrecy
  • Military buildup hurts secrecy, especially home defenses.
  • Big buildings hurt secrecy
  • Funding models go way up after the project is public. But you only have a limited number of rounds before riots break down the project.
  • Expanding research choices decreases secrecy. That makes that a harder choice.
  • Aliens can also decrease secrecy as part of their events or discovery consequences
  • You can increase secrecy again if you assassinate people. Or save a secrecy bump if you have an assassin.
